Federation isn't open just yet.

Coordinating a community's compute into a shared cloud means operators sign and verify everything they do. We're holding that door until the network's cryptography is future-proof.

What we're waiting for. Every operator key and signature moves to ML-DSA-65 — the NIST post-quantum signature standard — for all keys across the federation. That lands with the Go 1.27 release (expected 2026), when the stdlib ships it. We won't open real federation on Ed25519 that a future quantum computer could unwind.

Until then, enrollment (apply → register a 7-key ML-DSA-65 set → prove conformance) stays closed. Today's build runs on Ed25519 for development only.
