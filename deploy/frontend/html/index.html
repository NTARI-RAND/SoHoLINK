What it is

SoHoLINK is a cloud orchestration system that links communities of entrepreneurs — helping people create and lease cloud resources out of their own home-based hardware.

How it works

Three layers, from the screen in front of you to a cloud spread across cities.

01

The interface · CloudyThe app your community actually uses.

Cloudy is the original user interface to the SoHoLINK network. It runs on the SohoCloud Protocol (SCP) and ships under a copyleft license, so you can freely modify and rebrand it for your own community.

Copyleft · fork & rebrand Fork Cloudy on GitHub
02

The protocol · SCPThe rules that make a stranger's computer safe to use.

The SohoCloud Protocol lets individual owners lease their CPU, GPU, storage and software as services to anyone on the network — safely and securely. Host websites, facilitate settlements, and coordinate hardware across the internet.

CPU · GPU · Storage · Services Read the protocol spec
03

The federation · SoHoLINK.orgWhere separate communities become one cloud.

SoHoLINK.org tests and registers user interfaces into a federation of computer power. This is what lets individual computers replace datacenters: when multiple cities link their compute through the orchestrator, the network gets datacenter-scale without a datacenter.

Protections

Contributing your machine can't cost you your privacy.

Every way a computer helps the network is walled off from its owner's data and life. Here's what protects each one.

Data

Encrypted before it ever leaves your device.

Files are scrambled on the owner's own machine, then split into fragments spread across many computers. No single host ever holds a whole file — and none can read the pieces it stores.

Processing

Sealed in its own container, never touching your machine.

Each job runs isolated from your files and everything else on the computer. You choose exactly what you share — and the work pauses the instant you sit back down. The owner is never interrupted.

Print

Shared without opening up your network.

Printing is matched to your device through a local allow-list that you control. Nothing reaches your printer that you haven't cleared, and the network never sees the rest of your home.

SaaS, inverted

You don't pay the cloud. The cloud pays you.

Software as a service, turned around: rather than sending money to a corporation for hosting, the individual computer owners doing the hosting are the ones who get paid.
